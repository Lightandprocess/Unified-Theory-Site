The Light-Time-Consciousness Unification Theory

By Aaron Miller

Abstract

This theory proposes a novel unification of fundamental concepts: light as timeless, consciousness as the universe's means of self-observation, and dark matter as 'dead light.' By integrating ideas from relativity, quantum mechanics, and philosophy, it provides a new framework for understanding reality, potentially bridging scientific and metaphysical thought.

1. Light is Immune to Time

Light does not experience time. From the photon’s frame of reference, a journey across the universe takes zero time. This timelessness implies that light may be a foundational carrier of information, memory, and state. Light behaves as though it exists across all time simultaneously, making it a candidate for the substrate through which universal constants and events are stored or replayed.

2. Consciousness as the Universe Observing Itself

Consciousness is proposed to be the mechanism through which the universe becomes aware of itself. Quantum entanglement is severed upon observation — an action tied to consciousness. In this theory, observation is not a passive event but an active component in shaping reality. Human consciousness, as an evolved function of observation, may serve as the universe’s most intricate mirror.

3. Dark Matter as Dead Light

Dark matter is reinterpreted as “dead light” — light that has lost its time-immune state through observation or entropic degradation. It no longer interacts electromagnetically but retains gravitational influence. This reframing links dark matter to the life-cycle of photons and gives a testable hypothesis: regions of high dark matter concentration may correlate with past high observational entropy.

4. White Hole–Black Hole Singularity Recycling

The theory describes a closed-loop cosmological system where black holes act as data consumers and white holes as ejectors of restructured data/light. This forms a cosmological information cycle — a memory loop — where light travels from singularity to observation and back. The singularity is not merely a point of compression, but a transition state within a larger memory-processing loop.

5. Light as a Data Carrier Across Dimensions

Light’s properties — speed, interference, polarization — suggest it carries more than energy: it may encode information. If observation breaks entanglement and collapses state, then each photon absorbed or deflected is a data transfer. In this theory, photons are the carriers of universal memory across time-free channels.

6. Mathematical Foundation

A proposed formulation:

Let:
L = Light (photon, time-immune energy)
T = Time (observer-local progression)
C = Consciousness (self-reflective observer)
D = Dead Light (dark matter)

Then:
L ⊗ C → T (Light entangled with consciousness creates time)
L - T → D (Light that is removed from time becomes dark matter)
∫L0∞ (C dt) = Universal Memory Loop

7. God as Light: A Philosophical Bridge

The theory touches on metaphysical parallels by noting that religious texts often describe God as light — omnipresent, timeless, all-seeing. If light is the substrate of timeless information and universal awareness, then “God is light” may be more than metaphor: it becomes a poetic expression of physical reality. This builds a conceptual bridge between spirituality and physics.
