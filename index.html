The Unified Theory of Universe Emergence

by LightandProcess

Overview

This theory proposes a cyclical cosmological model where black holes serve as gateways for the birth of new universes. Instead of leading to a singular end, each black hole initiates a transfer of energy and matter into a new cosmic domain— a white hole-like phenomenon, which we identify as a Big Bang event. The theory reimagines light as a foundational unit of the universe and suggests a novel interpretation of dark matter, gravity, and the nature of time.

Core Concepts

1. Light as the Singularity

Light is treated as the primary medium capable of infinite compression. Its ability to carry information, interact with gravitational fields, and convert into matter under extreme conditions (e.g., Bose-Einstein condensation) supports the idea that it could represent the singularity itself. Unlike matter, which has mass and volume, light can theoretically exist in a dimensionless, infinitely dense point.

2. Black Hole to White Hole Cycle

As black holes accumulate light and matter, they compress into a singular point. Upon reaching an energetic threshold and encountering an external medium (such as absolute zero space), a transformation occurs—ejecting energy and matter into a new spacetime domain via a white hole. This event mirrors the conditions of a Big Bang.

3. Emergence into Absolute Zero

The singularity emerges into a pre-existing void of absolute zero, aligning with theories that project the heat death of the universe. Light interacts with this medium, momentarily forming solid-state configurations before expanding explosively, birthing a new universe.

4. Time and Human Perception

The theory argues that time is not absolute but rather observed and interpreted by conscious beings. Human cognition enables the documentation and measurement of time, essentially manifesting the dimension through observation. Without observers, time lacks structure or reference.

5. Dead Light as Dark Matter

Dark matter is reimagined as dead light—photons devoid of usable information. This light, having passed through multiple cycles of compression and transformation, no longer interacts visibly but retains gravitational influence, explaining its observable mass effect on galaxies.

6. Gravity as Light Residue

Gravity may emerge from the interaction between planetary magnetic cores and ambient dead light. As Earth moves through space, its core attracts this residual light, contributing to gravitational forces. This could explain variations in gravitational strength across celestial bodies.

Unified Mathematical Model

A proposed unifying equation that abstracts the energy flow from singularity to universe: 


U = \lim_{T \to 0} \left[ \int_{0}^{\infty} cE(\lambda) , d\lambda + \int_{0}^{\infty} M(\rho)g , d\rho \right] \times \gamma

Where:

U = Total output energy of the universe

cE(λ) = Energy of light as a function of wavelength λ

M(ρ)g = Mass function interacting with gravity

T → 0 = Absolute zero conditions

γ = Relativistic and quantum effects near singularity

This equation combines quantum energy fields and relativistic mass-energy conversion under absolute zero emergence conditions.
