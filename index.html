The Light-Time-Consciousness Unification Theory

By Aaron Miller

Abstract

This theory proposes that light, time, and consciousness are interconnected in a foundational way that governs both the material universe and subjective experience. Light is framed as immune to time, consciousness as the universe observing itself, and dark matter as "dead light." These ideas are supported by conceptual models, philosophical reasoning, and metaphoric extensions of physics like relativity and quantum entanglement.

Introduction

The Light-Time-Consciousness Unification Theory suggests that the fundamental properties of light may be the key to understanding everything from the structure of space-time to the emergence of conscious observers. This framework is not just metaphysical speculation—it aims to provoke new lines of inquiry and unify several unsolved mysteries across physics and philosophy.

Light and Time

Photons, traveling at the speed of light, experience no passage of time due to relativistic effects. This gives light a unique position: it exists in all times simultaneously from its perspective. This property implies a mechanism by which the universe maintains a form of timeless memory—a cosmic ledger of information outside temporal constraints.

Consciousness as the Observer

Quantum mechanics tells us that the act of observation collapses wavefunctions. This suggests consciousness is not passive, but active in shaping reality. The theory extends this by proposing that consciousness is how the universe perceives itself—through living beings embedded in time.

Dark Matter as "Dead Light"

In this theory, dark matter is reframed as light that has lost its temporal immunity—light that has been observed, collapsed, or stripped of its energy and time-free state. This would elegantly account for its gravitational effects without any electromagnetic interaction, while linking it directly to known phenomena.

Black Holes, White Holes, and the Singularity Cycle

The theory postulates that black holes and white holes are two ends of a universal cycle. Light enters a black hole, exits a white hole, and this cycle preserves information. The singularity, then, is not the end, but a tunnel between universal states—a data bridge of cosmic regeneration.

Mathematical Model

A conceptual equation representing the interplay between light, time, and consciousness:


U = ∫(L × C) / T

Where: U = the evolving universe L = light (energy with zero rest mass and time immunity) C = consciousness (the observer effect, entanglement collapses) T = time (the entropy-driven ordering of information)

Cosmological Implications

This model offers a resolution to the information paradox, proposes a new candidate for dark matter, and presents a metaphysical bridge between the scientific and the spiritual. If light is omnipresent and time-immune, and consciousness is entangled with reality formation, then life itself is the universe's feedback loop—a self-reflective mechanism.

Theological Parallels

The phrase “God is light” appears in religious texts and philosophical musings. This theory, while scientific, doesn’t exclude such spiritual interpretations. Light as a timeless, universal, all-seeing, creative force has deep overlap with descriptions of divinity across cultures.
