Introduction

This theory proposes a unified framework in which the interplay between black holes, white holes, and light (as both energy and information) accounts for the emergence, recycling, and evolution of universes. It further proposes that consciousness serves as the observer and anchor of the time dimension.

Key Concepts

  • Black holes consume matter and light; white holes emit them.
  • Light may act as the singularity between the two.
  • Time is born from the presence of conscious observers.
  • Dark matter may be “dead light” — energy with no information.
  • Gravity could emerge from interactions with dead light attracted to planetary cores.
